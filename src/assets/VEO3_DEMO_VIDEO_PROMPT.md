## FULL PROMPT

```
Create a 45-60 second cinematic animated explainer video for "Aminy" - an AI companion mobile app that helps parents of children with autism navigate daily life with confidence.

===========================================
VISUAL STYLE & AESTHETIC
===========================================

Art Direction:
- Style: Modern 2.5D animation with subtle depth, similar to Headspace or Calm app videos
- NOT cartoonish or childish - this is for exhausted adults who want to feel respected
- Sophisticated, warm, emotionally intelligent
- Think: Apple product video meets Pixar emotional storytelling
- Clean lines, generous whitespace, thoughtful composition

Color Palette (MUST use these exact brand colors):
- Background: Soft cream white (#F5F5F5)
- Primary text/elements: Deep navy (#0D1B2A)
- Accent color (buttons, highlights): Muted teal (#577590)
- Soft mint for success/calm moments: (#C9EAD9)
- Warm amber for warmth/hope: (#FFE2B6)
- Gentle lavender for magic/AI moments: (#E6E0F8)

Typography Feel:
- Clean sans-serif (like Inter or SF Pro)
- Generous letter spacing
- Text should breathe - never cramped

Lighting:
- Soft, diffused natural light
- Warm color temperature
- Subtle ambient glow on interactive elements
- Golden hour warmth in hopeful moments

Animation Style:
- Smooth, gentle easing (ease-in-out)
- No jarring cuts or fast movements
- Transitions should feel like a slow exhale
- Micro-animations that feel alive but not distracting
- Everything moves with intention and calm

===========================================
CHARACTERS
===========================================

THE PARENT (Main Character):
- Gender: Female, but designed to feel universal/relatable
- Age: Early-to-mid 30s
- Appearance: Warm, relatable, slightly tired but loving eyes
- Hair: Practical ponytail or loose bun (the "I have kids" look)
- Clothing: Comfortable but put-together - soft sweater, leggings
- Expression range: Overwhelmed → Curious → Relieved → Confident → Hopeful
- Body language: Shoulders hunched (stressed) → gradually relaxing → standing tall
- Skin tone: Medium, warm undertones (universally relatable)
- NO perfect "Instagram mom" aesthetic - real, lived-in, authentic

THE CHILD:
- Age: Appears 4-6 years old
- Gender: Ambiguous/universal
- Appearance: Adorable but not in a saccharine way
- Shows subtle autism traits authentically:
  - Perhaps looking slightly away during interaction
  - Focused intensely on something they love
  - Stimming gently (hand flapping when happy - shown POSITIVELY)
- Clothing: Comfortable, sensory-friendly clothes
- Hair: Slightly messy (realistic)
- Expression: Ranges from overwhelmed to genuinely happy

AMINY (The AI):
- Visual representation: Soft, glowing orb or gentle presence
- Color: Gradient of mint, amber, and lavender (the AI gradient)
- NOT a robot, NOT a human avatar
- Feels like a warm, intelligent light
- Pulses gently when "speaking"
- Surrounds the phone screen with a subtle glow

===========================================
SCENE-BY-SCENE BREAKDOWN
===========================================

--- SCENE 1: THE STRUGGLE (0:00 - 0:12) ---

Setting: Modern, lived-in kitchen/living room. Morning light through windows. Toys visible but not overwhelming. A therapy schedule on the fridge.

Action:
- Open on close-up of parent's tired eyes, slight bags underneath
- Pull back to reveal: She's sitting at kitchen table, coffee getting cold
- Child is in background, having difficulty with getting dressed
- Child's shirt is inside-out, they're frustrated, starting to escalate
- Parent looks at the clock - 7:45 AM - and sighs deeply
- Her phone buzzes with calendar reminder: "Therapy 8:30 AM"
- She puts her head in her hands briefly

Visual details:
- Scattered ABA flashcards on counter
- Sensory toys visible
- Therapy appointment card on fridge
- Parent's phone showing 5 unread messages from "Mom Group"

Text overlay (appears gently):
"Every morning feels like a mountain"

Emotional beat: Recognition - viewers should think "that's me"

Sound design notes (for reference):
- Muffled morning sounds
- Child's frustrated sounds (gentle, not distressing)
- Clock ticking subtly

--- SCENE 2: DISCOVERY (0:12 - 0:20) ---

Setting: Same kitchen, parent picks up phone

Action:
- Parent takes a breath, picks up phone
- Opens Aminy app (show app icon briefly - soft teal with gentle curves)
- Screen illuminates her face with warm, soft light
- The Aminy glow (mint/amber/lavender gradient) gently surrounds the phone
- She types with her thumb: "Help - he won't get dressed"
- We see the chat interface briefly - clean, minimal, calming

Visual details of app interface:
- White background with soft shadows
- Rounded corners everywhere
- Teal accent color on send button
- Aminy's response appears with gentle typing indicator
- Message bubbles are spacious, not cramped

Text overlay:
"Meet Aminy"

Emotional beat: Curiosity, a glimmer of hope

--- SCENE 3: THE RESPONSE (0:20 - 0:32) ---

Setting: Phone screen becomes the focus, then we see parent's reaction

Action:
- Aminy's response appears (show actual helpful content):
  "I understand morning dressing can be really hard. Here's something that works for many families:

  Try giving him a 5-minute warning AND letting him choose between 2 shirts.

  The warning reduces transition surprise. The choice gives him control.

  Would you like me to set a visual timer for him?"

- Parent's expression shifts: eyebrows raise slightly, small nod
- She looks up at her child with new understanding
- She taps "Yes, set timer"
- A cute visual timer appears on screen (not childish, but clear)

Visual details:
- Show the AI response with proper formatting
- Small "Personalized for [Child's name]" badge
- Subtle sparkle/glow when helpful suggestion appears
- Parent's face lit by phone, expression softening

Text overlay:
"Personalized guidance in seconds"

Emotional beat: Relief, "why didn't I think of that?"

--- SCENE 4: THE TRANSFORMATION (0:32 - 0:45) ---

Setting: Same room, but feeling warmer now. Time has passed (clock shows 8:05).

Action:
- Parent approaches child calmly, kneels to their level
- Holds up two shirts: "Blue rocket or green dinosaur?"
- Child considers, points to one (stimming happily with other hand)
- Child puts on shirt THEMSELVES
- Small victory moment - they look at each other and smile
- Parent's shoulders visibly relax
- They walk toward door together, child holding parent's hand

Visual details:
- Morning light is now golden, warmer
- The chaos from before is slightly tidied
- Child wearing chosen shirt proudly
- Visual timer on phone shows "Done! Great job!"
- Parent slips phone in pocket with a small smile

Quick montage within this scene (3-4 seconds):
- Calendar with therapy appointments (managed, not overwhelming)
- Progress chart showing upward trend
- Parent and child doing a fun activity together (following Aminy suggestion)
- Bedtime going smoothly
- Parent actually drinking coffee while it's hot

Text overlay:
"Small changes. Real progress."

Emotional beat: Hope, empowerment, "I can do this"

--- SCENE 5: THE CLOSE (0:45 - 0:55) ---

Setting: Cozy evening scene. Living room, soft lamp light.

Action:
- Parent sits on couch, child asleep on her lap
- She looks down at sleeping child with pure love
- She glances at phone - Aminy app showing:
  "Today's wins:
  ✓ Successful morning routine
  ✓ Tried new food at lunch
  ✓ Calm bedtime"
- She smiles softly, closes her eyes briefly
- Camera slowly pulls back through window
- House looks warm against evening sky
- Other houses visible - she's not alone

Text overlay (final message):
"You're not doing this alone anymore"

Hold for 2 beats, then:

--- SCENE 6: END CARD (0:55 - 1:00) ---

Setting: Clean, cream background

Action:
- Aminy logo fades in center (simple, elegant)
- Logo: The word "Aminy" in deep navy, clean sans-serif
- Subtle AI gradient glow pulses once behind it
- Tagline appears below: "Your AI companion for the autism journey"

Final text:
"Start free today"
"No credit card required"

Logo and text hold for 3 seconds, gentle fade to cream.

===========================================
CRITICAL EMOTIONAL NOTES
===========================================

What this video MUST feel like:
- A warm hug from someone who truly understands
- Permission to breathe
- "Finally, someone gets it"
- Hope without toxic positivity
- Competence, not pity

What this video must NEVER feel like:
- Preachy or condescending
- "We'll fix your broken child"
- Medical or clinical
- Overwhelming or adding pressure
- Fake or performative positivity
- Inspiration porn

Autism representation guidelines:
- Show autism as a difference, not a deficit
- The child is the same child before and after - just better supported
- Stimming is shown neutrally or positively, NEVER as something to stop
- The child's personality shines through
- Parent is not "heroic" - they're human and learning

The parent's journey:
- From: Isolated, overwhelmed, doubting herself
- To: Supported, confident, connected to her child
- NOT to: "Perfect parent" - still human, still learning

===========================================
TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS
===========================================

Format: MP4
Resolution: 1920x1080 (16:9) preferred, also create 1080x1920 (9:16) vertical version
Frame rate: 30fps
Duration: 45-60 seconds (55 seconds ideal)
Aspect ratio: Create both horizontal (YouTube) and vertical (Instagram/TikTok)

===========================================
ALTERNATE SHORTER VERSION (30 sec)
===========================================

If you need a 30-second cut:
- Scene 1: 0:00-0:06 (shortened struggle)
- Scene 2-3 combined: 0:06-0:16 (discovery + response)
- Scene 4: 0:16-0:24 (transformation montage)
- Scene 5-6: 0:24-0:30 (close + end card)

===========================================
MUSIC/SOUND DIRECTION (for reference)
===========================================

Opening: Quiet, slightly tense, isolated piano note
Middle: Gentle hope building, soft strings joining
Transformation: Warmth blooming, fuller arrangement
Close: Peaceful resolution, return to simplicity
Style: Similar to Nils Frahm or Ólafur Arnalds - minimal, emotional, not manipulative

===========================================
ITERATION PROMPTS
===========================================

If the first generation isn't right, try these refinements:

"Make the parent look more tired in the opening scene"
"The child should look more authentically autistic - include subtle stimming"
"Warm up the color temperature throughout"
"Slow down all transitions by 20%"
"Make the Aminy glow more subtle and sophisticated"
"The ending should feel more hopeful without being cheesy"
"Add more golden hour lighting to the transformation scene"
```

## USAGE NOTES

### For Best Results:

1. **Run the full prompt first** - Veo 3 handles long prompts well
2. **Iterate on specific scenes** - If Scene 1 is perfect but Scene 4 needs work, you can refine
3. **Check brand colors** - AI sometimes drifts; compare against the hex codes
4. **Watch for autism stereotypes** - Reject any generation that shows autism negatively

### What to Accept:
- Warm, hopeful tone
- Parent who looks real (tired but loving)
- Child who is clearly loved
- Calm, sophisticated animation
- Authentic emotion

### What to Reject and Re-generate:
- Parent who looks "perfect" or unrealistic
- Child depicted stereotypically or negatively
- Animation that feels cheap or rushed
- Colors that don't match brand palette
- Anything that feels manipulative or preachy
- Medical/clinical aesthetic

### After Generation:

1. Download the MP4
2. Upload to YouTube (unlisted first to test)
3. Get the embed URL: `https://www.youtube.com/embed/VIDEO_ID`
4. Update `OnboardingEnhanced.tsx`:
   ```tsx
   const DEMO_VIDEO_URL = 'https://www.youtube.com/embed/YOUR_VIDEO_ID';
   ```
5. Rebuild and test

---

## ALTERNATIVE: Screen Recording Approach

If Veo 3 doesn't produce what you need, a screen recording works great:

1. Open Aminy on your phone
2. Start screen recording
3. Type: "My son won't get dressed in the morning"
4. Show Aminy's response
5. Show tapping a suggestion
6. End on dashboard with progress
7. 30-45 seconds total
8. Add soft music in post

This is often MORE effective because it shows the REAL app.
